Hi Choi,

1. yes, but you might get into race conditions when registration happens
in parallel, with the authentication vectors downloaded by the S-CSCF
from the HSS. In theory it should be OK, but in practice it might not.
According to the standards, I would avoid this.

2. In any case, a private identity MUST be configured in the IMS core.
The way that we deal with this is that we are actually provisioning a
private identity equal to the public one without the "sip:" scheme. With
most of the current SIP clients you can manage to then provision them
somehow so that this would work properly.

3. The private identity identifies an authentication endpoint. The
public identity identifies the user serviced by an UE and the contact
the location of a n UE. But just saying "UE" is too vague to link it to
anything.

Cheers,
-Dragos
