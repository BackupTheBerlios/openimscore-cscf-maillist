Hi Jason,

 

There is no sound reason behind this architecture. AFAIK the standardization
team "forgot" to include an Ro interface in S-CSCF. The IMS GWF was the
remedy to this problem.

In a real implementation the IMS GWF usually is included in the same box as
a the S-CSCF, not an extra box.

 

At least this is the story I have heard.

Hi All,

 

I have been trauling through the 3GPP IMS specs, specifically the ones
related to Online Charging (OCS) and am puzzled by the architecture:

 

Why is it mandatory that all online charging requests are proxied to an IMS
GWF (which for all intents and purposes is an app server / SLEE of some
sort). Surely it would be more efficient to let the sCSCF talk Diameter (Ro
interface) to the OCS directly, as the SCSCF is already session aware and is
the master of the call. I am battling to find any reasoning behind this
architecture. I also find it crazy to route every single call in an IMS
network through a SIP AS, simply for billing purposes. Sure, if there are
additional 3rd party / supplementary services then I see how we could use
the IMS GWF, BUT for basic calling, surely this is inefficient?

 

I am interested in your thought on this topic and what you think the value
of adding an Ro interface to sCSCF would be (which I am quite happy to do)?

 

Kind regards

Jason
