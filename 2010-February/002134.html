> 
> I looked explicitly for the ports and could not find some definitive
> mentions for registration.
> 
> Basically at registration the P-CSCF can subscribe through Rx to the
> signaling path status and to the IP-CAN changes, so that it can act
> accordingly on events like loss of bearer.
> 
> Then, logically, I think that it makes sense to explicitly specify
flows
> for signaling, in the same operation, so that QoS reservation and/or
> gate opening can happen. Not including the ports here seems then a bit
> senseless as one can't differentiate the sinaling. In EPC there's such
a
> default bearer allocated per subscriber on attachment, targeted at any
> signaling. But this is just in the case of EPC and also even there it
> seems a bit too general. It would be better if one can (if needed)
> differentiate and provide better signaling conditions to IMS
> authenticated users, so this is why I insist of adding flows on
> registration for the SIP signaling. Functionally, they should be very
> similar to what happens on the calls and the PCC components don't need
> to handle it differently.
> 
> I am not really sure if the issue with the UCT PCRF is actually the
> ports, but in any case, we'll put in some configuration flags to keep
it
> also backwards compatible and not break functionality.
