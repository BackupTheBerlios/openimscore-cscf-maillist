> Hi Min,
> 
> In any case, neither the Open IMS Core nor Open EPC had or will have
at
> any point the target to compete with the industry or any of the big
> players that you mentioned. It would be anyway a futile attempt, as
such
> complex systems require huge investments to get to carrier-grade-ready
> implementations. But industrial partners find it beneficial if the
> technology adoption happens faster. Also they can have an early look
at
> what is involved so they can do very early positioning and
optimizations.
> 
> Our scope, as always, is to help the industry on the path towards the
> NGN/NGMN. And here I would have to go a bit specific targets:
> - The operators can try for themselves the concepts and technologies,
> without having to commit millions. Also they can directly influence
the
> technologies by doing  own evaluations, in-house changes and demanding
> that those are included into standards and new products. As such, for
> operators, we are deploying open and independent test-beds and then
> further help them understand, deploy and maintain their networks on
the
> migration to IP.
> - The equipment manufacturers have the means to trial their R&D
efforts
> and standardization directions without committing big budgets to new
> technologies. Also having independent prototypes are a great help in
> developing and validating individual components (e.g. developing a
> specific IMS Application Server).
> - The academia can use it to educate students in a direct hands-on
> approach. If the students can be exposed directly to the
inner-workings
> of the NGNs, in a vendor-independent environment, then they will much
> better comprehend the telecom technologies. And here there is a great
> potential for innovation.
> 
> Although the advantages of open source are huge, at this point we
can't
> really bring-up something as big as the OpenEPC in the same model.
Even
> if not open source, with OpenEPC we're maintaining the same "Open"
> prefix because we're looking for providing open interfaces and
> configuration. We'll be looking at technologies from a generalized
point
> of view and try to put in what is requested and leave out the
> standardization artifacts. We will also give back our experiences to
the
> community that helped us a lot with the Open IMS Core and we promise
to
> compete fairly where open source alternatives exist.
> 
> I don't know if I put the situation into light best by with
> "fragmented". Well, I am a bit concerned that we'll have to face
similar
> issues that we faced when we introduced the Open IMS Core... like the
> reluctance from operators to invest into it, although alternative
> commercial testbeds are not available or priced much, much higher. Or
> misunderstanding from TEMs that consider us competition, although
we're
> actually accelerating adoption.
> 
> Cheers,
> -Dragos
