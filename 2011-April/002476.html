First of all, you should disable logging as this has a tremendous effect on performance. Then increase the has sizes to an order of magnitude close to your number of subscribers and parallel dialogs. Also give it more RAM.

Then use lots of users, as we have designed it for a realistic use case, not 100 calls/sec/subscriber.

Then you will need a better HSS as FHoSS is a bit slugish due to Java.

I managed to drive it to 250 regs/sec and this is by far the most intensive operation. I used then CHeSS, but this is not yet open source (now part of openepc.net).

In operations that don't use the HSS, you should see hundreds of cps and even thousands of simple message exchanges. You can get even more if you use multiples systems and multiple CSCFs, as for all my tests I used a single system running all components. Was a dual quad-core Xeon @ 2.33GHz workstation with plenty of RAM.

Cheers,
