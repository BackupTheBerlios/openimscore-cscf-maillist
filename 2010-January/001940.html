Well, it's a mix of funding on our side. It's mainly industry money, yet 
I can not also rule out state money as we do obtain grants, although not 
directly for this project. There is no roadmap now, the project being 
driven rather by its users and contributors as they need and expand it.

We (as FOKUS) are using it as a core network for our R&D work on NGN, 
SOA and lately also NGMN. But commercially (in the sense of running a 
telecom network and charging subscribers for the usage) we are not and 
it is certainly out of our scope. There's a whole list of patents that 
you'll probably want to dodge if you'd use it like that, some of them as 
ridiculous as establishing calls by using proxies.

But these is valid however with any IMS core network. The Open IMS Core 
is licensed under the GPL license, which does not restrict your rights 
to use it in any way you want. That being said, although many parties 
have been interested into doing this, I am not aware of anybody actually 
doing it.

Cheers,
-Dragos
